Gorkha Earthquake

On 25 April 2015, a 7.8 magnitude earthquake struck Nepal,
Its epicentre was 81km north-west of the capital Kathmandu.
It was followed on 12 May by a 7.3 magnitude earthquake
that struck the district of Dolakha, leading to further loss of
life and infrastructure damage, increasing the humanitarian
needs.

Effect​s

On 12 May 2015, a 6.8 ml strong aftershock caused further damage and sufferings. These earthquakes took the lives of 8970 people where 200 people were missing, and 22,300 people were seriously injured. The earthquakes completely  destroyed 605,000 houses and 289,000 houses were partially damaged. It is estimated that the total value of the damages caused by the earthquakes is US$ 7 billion. Around 800,000 people who were displaced by the earthquake in Nepal struggled to survive in the severe lack of safe housing.

This earthquake taught the world a lesson, the earthquake was not the worst part, rather the poorly built structure and the aftermath of the earthquake was the worst part. In Nepal, the earthquake killed 9000 people and displaced a total of 600,000 people. Showing that the poorly built house is a significant cause in the need for support from other countries.

The hazardous effects do not only rely on the magnitude on the Richter scale or intensity alone, but also on so many factors, such as geology of the earth crust (lithology, elasticity, soil condition, permissible stress, rock structure, etc.) 

Cause

The Nepal earthquake was caused by a Destructive Margin. The Indian plate, the more dense plate boundary sunk beneath the less dense plate boundary, the Eurasian plate. It created intense pressure, high friction, and the rocks in between got stuck, therefore causing a massive earthquake in Nepal.  

Rescue

The response included the participation of many United Nations agencies and other international humanitarian organisations like the ICRC, IFRC, MSF, Oxfam, World Vision, CARE international, International Medical Corps, and Save the Children. There were many smaller NGOs and private sector commitments that significantly contributed to the relief effort.
